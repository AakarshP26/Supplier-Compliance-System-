## Structure

### Abstract (≈200 words)
- LLM-based supplier risk scoring is being adopted; literature trusts the
  evidence ecosystem implicitly.
- We introduce *evidence-source poisoning*: the supplier-under-evaluation
  injects synthetic positive content on low-credibility channels.
- Empirically, on 25 Indian electronics suppliers, modest budgets (B≥7)
  drive the F1 of a vanilla pipeline to **zero**.
- We propose a trust-calibrated multi-source Dempster–Shafer fusion with
  burst and template-similarity downweighting; this restores F1 to 1.0
  on the same attack while preserving clean-data performance.
- All code, data, and prompts are released.

### 1. Introduction (≈1 page)
- The supply-chain LLM arc: Liu & Meidani 2024 → RAG-for-procurement →
  LLM-as-decision-support.
- The unstated assumption: the ecosystem the LLM consumes is honest.
- Our threat model challenges that assumption directly.

### 2. Related work (≈1 page)
- LLM applications in supplier risk (Liu & Meidani 2024; Kumar et al. 2024).
- Adversarial robustness of LLMs (jailbreaks, prompt injection,
  data poisoning) — show the gap.
- Astroturfing / review-manipulation literature — closest analogue but
  not formalised for compliance scoring.
- Dempster–Shafer in trust-aware fusion (cited but rare in supply-chain).

### 3. Threat model (≈1 page)
- Adversary, capability, budget, vectors (press-release, anon-blog,
  self-published).
- Out-of-scope: cannot modify government lists or tier-1 news.
- Goal: raise score above operator's risky/safe threshold.
- Distinct from prompt injection (no model access) and from review fraud
  (target is a structured scorer, not a human reader).

### 4. Method (≈2 pages)
- 4.1 Provenance-aware evidence schema.
- 4.2 Source credibility prior (the table from `credibility.py`).
- 4.3 Dempster–Shafer fusion with Yager's modification — equations.
- 4.4 Defense: temporal burst penalty + template-similarity penalty.

### 5. Evaluation (≈2 pages)
- 5.1 Dataset: 25 Indian electronics suppliers (Table A1 in supplement).
- 5.2 Metrics: accuracy, F1, flip rate, mean lift, ECE.
- 5.3 Headline result (Table 1, regenerated by `make eval`).
- 5.4 Budget sweep (Figure 2, regenerated by `make sweep`).
- 5.5 Limitations: very small budgets bypass the burst threshold; very
  large attacks on multi-domain anon blogs partially evade.

### 6. Discussion (≈1 page)
- Why credibility priors are not enough on their own.
- Generalisation: any LLM-extractor system that fuses public unstructured
  signals is vulnerable in the same way.
- Future work: temporal anomaly detection in news streams, learned
  source-reputation, multi-tier supply chain extension.
